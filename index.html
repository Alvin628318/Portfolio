About Alvin

A composer from Taiwan. I write music that blends classical and romantic style/form with contemporary color. My most recent piece is my Symphony No.1 in A Major, first movement (Vivace). I'm also a pianist and percussionist. Contact me at alvinlinpersonal@gmail.com

FAQ

Q: What inspired you to start composing?
A: In elementary school, we had a project where we had to come up with a graduation song. I came up with a melody that the class voted as the best. That was when I figured that I actually could write music myself.

Q: When did you start composing?
A: I started playing around with my piano and MuseScore(Notation software) when I was around 13~14. At the time, I made mostly unfinished pop-style songs. I started seriously composing when I was 16. Since then, I made several pieces in both classical and pop style.

Q: How old are you now?
A: 17 - last year of high school.

Q: Which 3 composers do you like the most?
A: (In alphabetical sequence) Beethoven, Dvorak, Mozart.

Q: Which contemporary composer do you admire the most?
A: Toby Fox.

Q: Which piano piece do you enjoy the most?
A: Beethoven: Piano Sonata No. 8 in C minor "Pathetique", Op. 13

Q: Which symphony movement do you enjoy the most?
A: Dvorak: Symphony No. 8 in G Major, Op. 88: II. Adagio

Q: Who do you think, out of all due respect, is the most overrated composer?
A: Chopin. (Personal opinion, don't get mad)

Q: Out of all music ever conceived, which one piece is your favorite?
A: Mozart: Clarinet Concerto in A major, K. 622

Q: Is the four pieces displayed here the only works you've composed?
A: No. I've composed a few more complete pieces. I also have quite a lot of unfinished works, ranging from partly finished to merely just a melodic idea/theme. In the end, I chose four of my best completed works up for show.

Q: Which one of your pieces are you the most proud of?
A: My latest piece: Symphony No.1 in A Major - I. Vivace.

Q: How good do you think you are, in terms of compositional skill?
A: Somewhat good, but absolutely terrible compared to musical giants like Beethoven.

Q: Why did you use MS Basic as the sound library for The Summer Dream instead of the objectively better sounding MuseSounds?
A: When I started the piece, I didn't know that MuseSounds existed (Or it just didn't at the time). So when I heard about MuseSounds, I still finished the piece in MS Basic because the dynamics, articulations, and general sounds are entirely different from MuseSounds. If I were to switch to MuseSounds instead, I would've needed to change the instrumentation and re-orchestrate the entire piece in order to make it sound the same as before. I didn't want that, and so I proceeded with MS Basic.

Q: How do you feel about AI in music?
A: While AI is beneficial in many other ways (Including assisting me, a novice coder, in building this very website), I firmly believe that artifical intelligence has absolutely no place in any human arts, including music. I did not, and will not ever use any AI in any of my music, directly or indirectly, under any circumstances.

Q: Are you open to any commissions?
A: If you don't mind my currently immature music composition skills, then feel free to contact me through my email listed above, and I'll try my very best.
